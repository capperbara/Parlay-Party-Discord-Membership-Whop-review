## Understanding Sports Betting Fundamentals

  

Before diving into Parlay Party's specific approach, it's important to understand the types of bets they typically recommend.

  

### Single Game Bets

  

**Moneyline bets** are the simplest form of sports wagering.

  

You're simply picking which team will win the game outright, with no point spread involved.

  

Favorites pay lower odds while underdogs offer higher payouts to entice bettors.

  

**Point spread betting** is the most common type of NFL wager.

  

The spread handicaps one team and favors another when there's a perceived difference in skill level.

  

For example, if the Kansas City Chiefs are 7-point favorites over the Denver Broncos, they need to win by 8 or more points to cover the spread.

  

**Over/under bets** focus on the total combined score of both teams.

  

You're betting whether the actual total will be higher or lower than the number set by oddsmakers.

  

### Multi-Game Combinations

  

**Parlays** combine multiple bets into one wager with a much higher potential payout.

  

All bets in the parlay must win for you to collect - if even one loses, the entire parlay fails.

  

This is Parlay Party's specialty, and they account for over 70% of sportsbook revenue in most states because of their appeal to bettors.

  

**Teaser bets** are a type of parlay that gives you more favorable point spreads in exchange for a lower payout.

  

You might get an extra 6 points on each game in your teaser, making it easier to win but reducing your potential profit.

  

### Specialty Bets

  

**Proposition bets** focus on specific outcomes within a game rather than the final score.

  

Examples include betting on how many passing yards a quarterback will throw or which player scores the first touchdown.

  

**Futures bets** are long-term wagers on season-long outcomes like Super Bowl winners or division champions.

  

**Live betting** allows you to place wagers while games are in progress, with odds that change based on what's happening in real-time.

  

## Legal Considerations for Sports Betting

  

Before joining any betting community, you need to understand the legal landscape in your area.

  

**The Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA)** previously made sports betting illegal in most US states until 1992.

  

Only Delaware, Montana, Nevada, and Oregon were grandfathered in with existing sports lottery frameworks.

  

**The Supreme Court struck down PASPA in 2018** in the landmark Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association case.

  

This opened the door for individual states to legalize and regulate sports betting.

  

As of January 2024, **37 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico** have operational legal sports betting.

  

**30 states plus D.C. and Puerto Rico** also allow online sports betting, while 8 states restrict betting to physical sportsbook locations only.

  

Since the PASPA decision, over $20 billion has been wagered on sports in the United States as of May 2020, and that number has grown significantly since then.

  

**Always verify that sports betting is legal in your specific jurisdiction** before placing any wagers or joining betting communities.

  

Laws can vary not just by state, but by county and city as well.
